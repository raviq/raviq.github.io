I am a research fellow in the Department of Psychology (and Philosophy) at Monash University since April 2017.
My current work is at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence, Neuroscience and Philosophy, studying how cognitive abilities rise in humans, animals, and machines. I am particularly working on 4E cognition, active inference, and the integrated information theory of consciousness.

Prior to joining Monash, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Nagoya Institute of Technology in Japan. I worked on agent-based decision-making and large-scale multiagent simulation.

I am interested in the following topics (amongst other things):

  • Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) and formal model of general intelligence.
  • Enactive cognition and constructivist epistemology.
  • Multiagent simulation of complex systems: road traffic, weather conditions, motion, etc.
  • Consensus algorithms and distributed ledgers.
  • Automated negotiation with nonlinear preferences.
  • Machine Consciousness.
