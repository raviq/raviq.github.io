In December 2020, I joined the Department of Social Informatics at Kyoto University.

From September 2019 to December 2020, I was a researcher at the Nagoya Institute of Technology. I was developing conversational AI using Natural Language Processing/Generation, Sentiment Analysis, and Formal Argumentation. From April 2017, I was a research fellow in the departments of Psychology and Philosophy at Monash University. My work was at the intersection of AI, Neuroscience, and Philosophy. I mainly worked on 4E cognition, active inference, and the integrated information theory (IIT) of consciousness.

I am interested in the following topics:

  • Conversational multiagent systems and their applications to online deliberation and voting.
  • Multiagent simulation of complex systems, focusing on road traffic, urbanism, and social networks.
  • Automated decision-making, focusing on preference elicitation and automated negotiation.
  • Enactive AI, 4E cognition, autonomy, consciousness, and constructivist epistemology.
  • Artificial general intelligence (AGI) and formal models of intelligence.
