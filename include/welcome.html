I am an Associate Professor in the Graduate School of Informatics at Kyoto University in Japan. I joined the university in December 2020. From September 2019 to December 2020, I was a researcher at the Nagoya Institute of Technology. I was developing conversational AI for online deliberation. From April 2017, I was a research fellow in the departments of Psychology and Philosophy at Monash University in Australia.

Sporadically working on

  • Multiagent systems and their applications to democratic deliberation, voting, and digital inclusion.
  • Multiagent simulation of complex systems, road traffic, urbanism, and social networks.
  • Automated decision-making, preference elicitation and automated negotiation.
  • Enactive AI, 4E cognition, autonomy, agency, consciousness, and constructivist epistemology.
  • Artificial general intelligence (AGI) and formal models of intelligence.
